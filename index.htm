Solstice Time: Circling The Great Hood

A journey through the cultured wilderness of Oregon by Robert Hempaz, PhD Trichometry™

June 21st, 2015 | 17:30 PDT

The background photo of this home page is a snap taken with an Amazon Fire phone at approximately 7:30 pm in the evening in June with the time and sky quickly approaching the summer solstice at Mt Hood, Oregon USA.

Notice the north face of the mountain, a glacier reflecting the late night bright sunshine just south of the latitude of the Oregon - Washington State border.

At the foothills of the volcano (now extinct) lay hectares of cherry orchards giving up their fruit by the bushels. All along the roadway circling the great Mount Hood were crates and crates of freshly picked cherries stacked and ready for pick-up.

The deafening silence of the late evening's light and beauty was punctuated with the roar of the Hood river below.

And, the chirps of many species of songbirds coming home to roost for the night.

Directions: From the Oregon - Washington State border head east from Portland on I-84 hugging the south bank of the Columbia river. Drive past the Bonneville dam exits and then go south on Oregon State Highway 35 to US Highway 26.

Enjoy the view!
